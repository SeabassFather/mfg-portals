Platform Guide

How to Use Your LOAF Mini-Office

A complete operational walkthrough of every module and feature in the LOAF agricultural trade platform. Built for growers, packers, shippers, buyers, wholesalers, and brokers operating in the US–Mexico fresh produce corridor.

Overview

What is LOAF?

LOAF — the Launch, Origin, Altruistic, Factor Mini-Office — is the public-facing trade interface of the AuditDNA agricultural intelligence platform. It gives growers, packers, shippers, buyers, and wholesalers a single location to manage fresh produce trading: uploading inventory, issuing calls for tender, accessing financing, and managing a complete book of business with full traceability.

Registration

Navigate to loaf.mexausafg.com and select your participant category: Grower/Packer, Buyer/Wholesaler/Importer, Shipper/Logistics, or Broker/Agent. Complete the registration form and upload your required certifications. Applications are reviewed within 3 business days.

First Login

Upon approval you receive login credentials by email. Set your permanent password and 4-digit security PIN. The PIN is required for financing applications and bulk actions. Your dashboard displays active inventory, open tenders, incoming bids, financing status, and recent transaction history.

Module O
O
Module O — Origin
Program Registration
For: Growers with Year-Round or Recurring Programs

Use Origin to register an ongoing supply program — not a single lot, but a recurring supply relationship. This creates a permanent verified grower profile that buyers can find and reference continuously.

Use This Module If:

You have a year-round program (continuous avocado supply, recurring berry program, ongoing tomato supply from a specific region). You want buyers to find your ongoing availability without you re-uploading every week. You are establishing a branded grower identity on the platform.

Verified Program Benefits
  • Green VERIFIED badge displayed on all your inventory listings
  • Priority placement in buyer commodity searches
  • Direct profile link shareable with your own buyer network
  • Eligible for expedited financing application review
  • Season calendar visible to buyers showing your peak availability windows
Module A
A
Module A — Altruistic
Call for Tender
For: Buyers, Wholesalers, Importers, Distributors

The Altruistic module is how buyers issue a formal, structured Call for Tender. Post what you need — the platform broadcasts immediately to every qualified grower in the network whose profile matches your specifications.

How to Issue a Call for Tender

Click A — ALTRUISTIC from your dashboard
Complete the tender formCommodity, variety, volume, grade required, delivery window, destination, and target price (optional).
Enable BROADCAST TO GROWERSCheck this box to immediately notify all matching growers in the network.
Click SUBMIT CALL FOR TENDER
What Happens After You Submit
  • Every matching grower receives an immediate notification with your tender specifications
  • Growers submit bids directly through the platform
  • Bids appear in your dashboard ranked by price, proximity, certification status, and relationship history
  • Request clarification from any bidder through the platform messaging system
  • Award the tender — a Purchase Order is generated automatically with full terms
  • Selected grower receives PO with all specifications, delivery terms, and payment conditions

Modules B & R
B
Module B — Bid
Live Auction
For: Growers listing lots / Buyers seeking spot product

Growers list a specific lot with a reserve price and auction duration. Buyers bid in real time. The system awards to the highest qualifying bidder when the auction closes. An auto-bid feature allows buyers to set a maximum and the system bids incrementally on their behalf.

R
Module R — Reverse
Reverse Auction
For: Buyers posting needs / Growers competing for business

The inverse of Module B. A buyer posts a specific need — commodity, volume, grade, delivery date — and growers compete openly with their best price. Use Reverse when you want market price discovery through open competition. Use Altruistic (Module A) when you want structured, confidential sealed bids evaluated by your team.

Platform Feature

Your Book of Business

Every action you take on the LOAF platform is logged to your book of business — your permanent operational record. Accessible from any device. Exportable to PDF or CSV at any time.

Book of Business Contents
  • All active and historical inventory listings with unique lot numbers and full specifications
  • Open and completed purchase orders with all terms and delivery confirmation
  • Bid history — every bid submitted and every bid received, with timestamps and outcomes
  • Financing applications, advance history, and collection records
  • Contact directory — your verified trading partners on the platform
  • Document vault — certifications, food safety records, inspection reports
  • Payment and transaction ledger with full audit trail
Platform Feature

Traceability — How It Works

Every transaction on LOAF generates an immutable chain of custody record that satisfies FSMA requirements, retailer audit standards, and FDA Traceability Rule Key Data Elements. Records cannot be edited after confirmation.

Transaction StepData Captured
Inventory UploadGrower ID, growing region, commodity, Traceability Lot Code, harvest/pack date, certifications, packing house
Bid / Tender MatchBuyer ID, bid terms, award confirmation, timestamp
Purchase OrderPO number, delivery terms, payment terms, both party signatures
Delivery ConfirmationDelivery date, receiver name and location, condition notes, temperature log if provided
Invoicing & PaymentInvoice date, payment terms, payment date, method, amount

In the event of a recall, the platform can immediately identify every buyer who received product from a specific lot, pull all chain of custody documentation, and generate a complete FDA-formatted response — in minutes.

Participation Requirements

Certification Requirements

Growers & Packers — Mexico-Based
  • SENASICA current certification (required for US export)
  • Phytosanitary certificate per shipment
  • GlobalG.A.P. or equivalent food safety certification (strongly preferred)
  • USDA AMS inspection documentation for imported lots
  • Cold chain documentation if temperature-sensitive commodity
Growers & Packers — US-Based
  • USDA Harmonized GAP or LGMA (California leafy greens mandatory)
  • FSMA Produce Safety Rule compliance — written food safety plan required
  • Water testing records current
  • Worker health and hygiene training documentation
Buyers & Wholesalers
  • PACA license required for buyers of PACA-covered commodities above de minimis
  • Traceability program documentation for retail and foodservice accounts
  • Banking references and trade credit history
