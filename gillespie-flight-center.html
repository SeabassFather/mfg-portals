About Gillespie Field

Gillespie Field (KSEE) is the oldest and largest of San Diego County's seven airports. Named for Marine Lieutenant Archibald H. Gillespie, the field began as a Marine Corps parachutist training facility during WWII in 1942 — and has grown into the region's premier general aviation hub.

Located at 1960 Joe Crosson Drive, El Cajon, CA 92020 — just 10 miles northeast of San Diego's CBD — KSEE operates a FAA control tower, multiple FBOs, flight schools, maintenance shops, avionics services, aircraft rentals, and the beloved Gillespie Field Cafe directly on the ramp.

NOTAMs & Operational Notes
NOISE ABATEMENT
TGL and jet departures strongly discouraged 2200–0700. Noise sensitive areas all quadrants. RWY 17 preferred noise abatement departure when ATCT closed. Contact Airport Operations 619-956-4800 or sdcountyairports.com for pattern info.
WILDLIFE ADVISORY
Coyotes and birds occasionally on and in vicinity of airport. Exercise caution on all movement areas.
TOWER CLOSED PROCEDURE
For CD when ATCT closed — contact SoCal Approach at 800-448-3724. CTAF 120.7. Activate REIL RWY 27R; PAPI RWYs 09L, 27R, 17 & 35; MIRL RWY 09L/27R & 17/35 on CTAF.
FORMATION FLIGHT
Formation flight available as traffic permits. Limited to no more than 3 aircraft per formation.

Fuel Transition Alert — UL94 & GAMI G100UL in California
2025 FAA RULING — CALIFORNIA
FAA ruled March 2025 that Santa Clara County violated federal grant assurances by banning 100LL at KRHV and E16. County ordered to restore 100LL. California SB 1193 sets 2031 deadline for leaded avgas phase-out. 100LL required until then.
GAMI G100UL SITES
KRHV (Reid-Hillview)
KWVI (Watsonville)
First unleaded 100-octane. STC required.
UL94 SITES
KRHV, KWVI, KCCR
Low-compression engines only.
Verify approval before use.
DEADLINE
Jan 1, 2031 — CA ban on leaded avgas. Federal FAA AIP Assurance 40 now requires 100LL availability at grant airports until then.
