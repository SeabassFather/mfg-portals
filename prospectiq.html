FLAG DEFINITIONS — WHAT EACH FLAG MEANS
NOD — Notice of Default
Lender has filed formal notice that borrower is in default. First step in foreclosure. Property is pre-foreclosure — typically 90+ days delinquent.
NTS — Notice of Trustee Sale
Foreclosure sale has been scheduled. Property is 21+ days from auction. Highest urgency — owner must act immediately or lose property.
TAX DEFAULT
Owner has not paid property taxes. County may sell tax lien certificate or initiate tax deed sale. Research delinquent amount and redemption deadline.
LIS PENDENS
Lawsuit pending that affects the property. Title cannot transfer cleanly until resolved. Research the underlying case — could be divorce, partition, contract dispute.
IRS FEDERAL TAX LIEN
IRS has recorded a lien against the property owner. Follows the property on sale — must be paid or subordinated. Can often be discharged or withdrawn if negotiated.
MECHANIC'S LIEN
Contractor, subcontractor, or supplier who was not paid has filed against the property. Priority depends on state law. Research amount and contest period before closing.
HOA DELINQUENT
Homeowners association dues are delinquent. HOA can lien or foreclose in some states. Call HOA for payoff amount — usually clearable at closing.
MTG DEFAULT
Borrower has missed mortgage payments but NOD not yet filed. Watch for acceleration to NOD. Often the best time to approach — owner still has time to negotiate.
MULTIPLE LIENS
Two or more liens recorded against the property. Each must be researched individually. Some may be junior liens that can be negotiated — others require full payoff.
CLEAR
No flags detected in public records scan. Clean title, no defaults, no liens. Property may still have title issues not in public records — always order prelim before purchase.
