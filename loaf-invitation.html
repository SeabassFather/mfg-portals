Who We Are

Built by Operators, for Operators

Mexausa Food Group, Inc. is a California-based agricultural trade company with deep roots in the US–Mexico produce corridor. We operate a year-round Hass avocado program sourcing directly from certified growers in Michoacán, Jalisco, and Nayarit — delivering to distribution centers, wholesalers, and retail networks across the continental United States.

Our understanding of this business comes from living it daily: the logistics, the pricing volatility, the compliance requirements, the financing gaps, and the communication failures that cost this industry millions of dollars every season. Out of that operational experience, we built AuditDNA — a full-stack agricultural intelligence, CRM, and trade finance platform — and LOAF, its public-facing mini-office.

This is not a startup experiment. This is infrastructure built by people who run produce programs. We are now opening this platform to qualified participants across the supply chain.

The Problem We Solve

The Infrastructure Gap in Fresh Produce

The fresh produce industry operates on razor-thin margins, extreme time sensitivity, and a fragmented communication infrastructure that has not meaningfully evolved in decades. Growers still call brokers who call buyers who call their warehouse managers. Excess inventory sits and rots because the right buyer was not reached in time. Buyers issue calls for tender through email chains and phone trees. Financing decisions take days when hours matter. Traceability documentation is scattered across three different systems — or worse, on paper.

We built LOAF to eliminate every one of those failure points.

Trade Finance

Working Capital Built Into the Platform

One of the most persistent barriers in agricultural trade is capital. Growers wait 30, 60, sometimes 90 days for payment. Buyers need inventory but cannot deploy capital before the deal closes. Both problems are solvable within the platform.

For Growers & Shippers
Accounts Receivable Factoring

Submit a PACA application pending invoice for review. Receive an advance on the invoice face value within 24 hours. The platform manages collection. No waiting 30 days. No separate lender to find.

For Buyers & Growers
Purchase Order Financing

Confirmed PO but need capital to fund procurement, packing, or freight? Submit the PO through the platform. Financing terms presented within 2 business days. Capital moves against the confirmed order.

All financing is tied to the underlying transaction record. Complete financial audit trail. PACA-compliant documentation from submission through collection.

Traceability & Compliance

Every Transaction Leaves a Complete Record

Every transaction on the LOAF platform generates an immutable chain of custody record: grower identity and location, commodity and variety, lot number, harvest date, certifications, chain of custody, delivery confirmation, and payment status. This is not a secondary reporting function — it is embedded in every workflow from the moment inventory is uploaded.

The platform's traceability records satisfy FSMA Produce Safety Rule requirements, retailer audit standards (SQF, BRC, GFSI), FDA Traceability Rule Key Data Elements, and PACA dispute documentation requirements.

In the event of a recall, the platform can immediately identify every buyer who received product from a specific lot, pull all chain of custody documentation, and generate a complete FDA-formatted response — in minutes, not days.

Participant Criteria

Who Belongs in This Network

This platform is built for professionals who operate at volume and take compliance seriously. We are not building a spot-market app for casual transactions. We are building infrastructure for the serious participants in this corridor.

Growers & Packers

Commercial operations in Mexico or the US with recurring volume, current food safety certifications, and established export or domestic distribution relationships.

Buyers & Wholesalers

Shippers & Logistics

Brokers & Agents

Trade intermediaries managing relationships between growers and buyers who want platform infrastructure to support those relationships at scale.

Participation Requirements
  • Business registration documentation (US entity or Mexican RFC equivalent)
  • PACA license or confirmation of PACA-exempt status where applicable
  • Current food safety certifications — GAP, LGMA, GlobalG.A.P., SENASICA, or equivalent — for growers and packers
  • Banking references and trade credit history for financing applications
  • Designated point of contact who will manage your account on the platform
  • Two trade references from verifiable commercial relationships in the past 12 months
